frequently asked questions About damp-proof

damp-proof is a tried and tested method or solution that helps shield your house from water damage, humidity and leakages. It is important to get your house waterproofed by experts because humidity, moisture or even monsoons can lead to water leakages or seepages, structural damage and growth of mould or fungus. It is also important to let experts evaluate your home and devise appropriate damp-proof solutions for you.

Priming before damp-proof is vital to the health of your home. A coat of primer protects your wall from humidity and moisture. It also provides a strong and even surface for better adhesion of damp-proof coatings. You can also opt for damp-proof primers and putty to add an extra layer of damp-proof.

damp-proof solutions or methods include cementitious damp-proof, liquid damp-proof membrane, bituminous coating damp-proof, bituminous membrane damp-proof and Polyurethane liquid membrane damp-proof. Other kinds of damp-proof solutions or chemicals include grouting and epoxy, polymers, acrylics, latex etc.

Regular damp-proof paints last from 6 months to about 24 months. However, Ribon paint No damp+ gives you up to a 12 year warranty, leaving you stress free about its durability and longevity in the long run.

It is very important to let damp-proof paints, primers or sealants dry for at least 24-72 hours. Because of the thick layers of application and multiple coatings, it is important for each coat to dry for at least 4-7 hours before the next application as well.

For horizontal and exterior walls, application of 2-3 coats is imperative for effective results. For interior walls and smaller areas, application of 1-3 coats is advisable.

The best possible way to choose the right damp-proof solution or paint is to consult with a professional painter. Book a visit with Ribon’s experts for a free site evaluation. It is also important to choose a paint that addresses the area of the damage or prevention. For example, paints are different for exterior surfaces like roofs, terraces and basements whereas there are different paints for bathrooms and interior damp areas.

As a homeowner, you need to evaluate various factors before choosing a damp-proof solution. Factors like cost, materials, product durability and maintenance, product application process, warranty and guarantee, weather conditions, type of surface etc. It is best to let professionals determine the right damp-proof solution for your home.
